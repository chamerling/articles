One more time this year, I will give some talks at the OW2 annual conference. I spoke about Petals and the Cloud in <a title="Mon talk OW2Con 2010 en vidéo" href="http://chamerling.org/2010/11/30/mon-talk-ow2con-2010-en-video/">2010</a> and <a title="Let’s talk at OW2Con 2011" href="http://chamerling.org/2011/10/11/lets-talk-to-ow2con-2011/">2011</a> and this year again, I will talk about it but not only:
<ol>
	<li><strong>Playing with SOA, EDA and Event Processing in the Cloud</strong>. I will introduce the <a href="http://www.play-project.eu" target="_blank">PLAY research project</a> which will soon become an OW2 project. (Hope to do a demo which works on low resolution this year...)</li>
	<li><strong>What's new in Petals?</strong> <a href="http://petals.ow2.org" target="_blank">Petals</a> is not only an Enterprise Service Bus, there are also cool tools around which help to integrate your services and run your processes in a distributed way. We will also give details about what is coming in the next months, we have many things in mind. This will be a join talk with <a href="https://twitter.com/VincentZurczak" target="_blank">@VincentZurczak</a></li>
	<li><strong>Development 2.0 @ OW2</strong>. Finally, as an active Technology Council member, I will share a talk with <a href="https://twitter.com/sauthieg" target="_blank">@sauthieg</a> and <a href="https://twitter.com/clementplop" target="_blank">@clementplop</a> to introduce our vision on a distributed/collaborative development platform.</li>
</ol>
We also have some talks planned around other <a href="http://linagora.com" target="_blank">Linagora</a> projects: <a href="https://twitter.com/jplorre" target="_blank">@jplorre</a> will introduce the OpenPaaS project and <a href="https://twitter.com/clementoudot" target="_blank">@clementoudot</a> will <a href="http://lemonldap-ng.org/welcome/" target="_blank">LemonLDAP</a>.

More details on the talks and sessions are available on the conference <a href="http://ow2.org/view/OW2Con-2012/" target="_blank">Web page</a>. Note that this event is free as well as the <strong>beer</strong> at the social event...
